5. Foreign buyer obligations

Thailand has specific rules for non-Thai buyers. By engaging our services you acknowledge:

5.1 Condominiums (freehold)

Foreigners may own condominium units freehold, provided the foreign quota in the building does not exceed 49% of total saleable area. Funds for the purchase must be transferred from abroad in foreign currency and converted in Thailand, with documentation (FET form) issued by the Thai bank.

5.2 Villas and houses (land)

Foreigners cannot own land in Thailand directly. Common legal structures used by foreign buyers include:

  • Leasehold — typically 30 years, with renewal options where contractually agreed
  • Thai limited company — must comply with current Thai company law; we work with lawyers to assess suitability
  • Thai spouse ownership — land owned by a Thai spouse, with specific declarations regarding source of funds

We will guide you through the options, but final legal structure is determined by your lawyer based on your circumstances.

5.3 Due diligence

You are responsible for ensuring full due diligence is performed on any property before signing or transferring funds. This includes title verification, encumbrances, building permits, environmental restrictions, and tax history. We will help coordinate this with qualified professionals but cannot replace independent legal counsel.
