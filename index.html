Legal Guide

Buying Property in Thailand
as a Foreigner

Two straightforward routes to ownership — we guide you through both with our in-house legal team.

Condominium
Freehold Ownership
  • Foreigners can own up to 49% of any condo building outright
  • Full ownership title deed (Chanote) — same rights as Thai nationals
  • Can resell, rent out, or leave to heirs freely
  • No annual land tax complications for foreign owners
  • Transfer process typically takes 1–2 days at the Land Department
Prices from ฿1,500,000 (~$42,000)
Villa / House
Leasehold Ownership
  • 30-year registered lease renewable twice — up to 90 years total
  • Thai company ownership option available for full land control
  • Full use and rental rights throughout the lease period
  • Lease registered at Land Department with full legal protection
  • Common structure used by thousands of foreign villa owners in Pattaya
Prices from ฿3,000,000 (~$83,000)

Our legal team will guide you through every step of the purchase process. From due diligence and title deed checks to Land Department transfer — we make buying in Thailand straightforward and stress-free for international buyers.
